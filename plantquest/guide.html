PSLE Science Study Guide: Photosynthesis

Primary 5 — Plant System / Cycles Theme (MOE Syllabus)

1. What is Photosynthesis?

Definition sentence (memorise this word for word!):

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food, using water, carbon dioxide, light and chlorophyll.

The four things a plant needs:

Need Where it comes from How it reaches the leaf
Water The soil Absorbed by the roots → carried up the stem → to the leaves
Carbon dioxide The air Enters the leaf through tiny holes called stomata
Light Usually the Sun Falls on the leaf; absorbed by chlorophyll
Chlorophyll Already inside the leaf The green substance in the leaf that traps/absorbs light

What photosynthesis makes: - Food (sugar / starch) — the plant's energy store - Oxygen — given off into the air

⚠️ Trap alert — light is NOT "food"! Never write "the plant gets food from the Sun" or "the plant absorbs food from the soil". - ❌ "The plant takes in food through its roots." - ✅ "The plant makes its own food by photosynthesis, using water, carbon dioxide, light and chlorophyll." - The roots absorb water and mineral salts — NOT food!

2. The Four Needs — Know Them Cold

Examiners test these by removing one need and asking what happens. If any one is missing, photosynthesis cannot take place, so the plant cannot make food and will eventually die.

If this is missing... Then...
Water No photosynthesis — the plant cannot make food
Carbon dioxide No photosynthesis — the plant cannot make food
Light No photosynthesis — the plant cannot make food
Chlorophyll (e.g. white parts of a leaf) No photosynthesis in that part — no food made there

⚠️ Trap alert: Chlorophyll is not a need that comes from outside — it is already in the leaf. Light is trapped by the chlorophyll. So write: "Chlorophyll traps/absorbs light for photosynthesis."

3. Why the Leaf is Perfect for its Job

The leaf is the main food factory of the plant.

Feature of a leaf Why it helps
Broad and flat Large surface area to absorb more light
Green (contains chlorophyll) Chlorophyll traps light for photosynthesis
Thin Carbon dioxide can reach the inner cells easily
Stomata (tiny holes) Allow carbon dioxide to enter and oxygen to leave

4. Why is Photosynthesis So Important? (super common question)

Reason 1 — Food for all living things: Green plants are producers — they are the only organisms that make their own food. All animals depend on plants for food, either directly (herbivores eat plants) or indirectly (carnivores eat animals that ate plants).

Reason 2 — Oxygen for all living things: Photosynthesis gives out oxygen, which humans and animals need for respiration (to release energy).

Reason 3 — Removes carbon dioxide: Photosynthesis takes in carbon dioxide from the air, helping to keep the amount of carbon dioxide in the air balanced.

Answer formula:

"Green plants photosynthesise to make food, which is a source of food for animals. Photosynthesis also gives out oxygen, which living things need for respiration, and takes in carbon dioxide from the air."

5. Photosynthesis vs Respiration (compare-and-contrast favourite!)

⚠️ Biggest trap in this topic: Plants DO respire! Plants respire all the time, day and night — just like animals. Photosynthesis happens only when there is light.

Photosynthesis Respiration
Who does it? Green plants only All living things (plants AND animals)
When? Only in the presence of light All the time, day and night
Takes in Carbon dioxide + water Oxygen + food
Gives out Oxygen + food (sugar) Carbon dioxide + water
Energy Stores energy in food Releases energy from food

Key exam sentence:

During the day, plants photosynthesise and respire. At night, plants only respire (no light, so no photosynthesis).

⚠️ Trap alert: Never write "plants breathe out oxygen at night" or "plants only respire at night". At night, plants take in oxygen and give out carbon dioxide (respiration only).

6. Classic Experiment Questions

(a) Testing a leaf for starch

If a leaf has been photosynthesising, it will contain starch. We test with iodine solution: - Starch present → iodine turns blue-black → photosynthesis took place. - Starch absent → iodine stays brown → photosynthesis did not take place.

(b) The "variegated leaf" question (green and white leaf)

Q: A variegated leaf (green in the middle, white at the edges) is tested for starch. Only the green part turns blue-black. Why? A: Only the green part contains chlorophyll, which is needed to trap light for photosynthesis. So only the green part made food (starch). The white part has no chlorophyll, so no photosynthesis took place there and no starch was made.

(c) The "covered leaf" question

Q: Part of a leaf is covered with black paper for 2 days, then tested for starch. Why does the covered part stay brown? A: The black paper blocked the light from reaching the covered part. Without light, photosynthesis could not take place there, so no starch was made.

(d) Fair test rules (very important!)

In any photosynthesis experiment, only ONE variable should change. Everything else must be kept the same (same type of plant, same amount of water, same temperature, same size of leaf, etc.).

⚠️ Trap alert: Before an experiment, a plant is often kept in the dark for 1–2 days first. Why? To remove any starch already stored in the leaves, so that any starch found afterwards must have been made during the experiment. (This is called destarching.)

7. Answering Techniques (Section B)

Must-use keywords: - "photosynthesis" / "makes its own food" - "water, carbon dioxide, light and chlorophyll" - "chlorophyll traps/absorbs light" - "carbon dioxide enters through the stomata" - "gives out oxygen" - "photosynthesis cannot take place, so no food is made" - "producer" / "release energy"

Missing-need answer formula:

"Without ___, photosynthesis cannot take place. The plant cannot make food, so it will not have enough energy to grow / it will eventually die."

Light/chlorophyll answer formula:

"That part of the leaf has no chlorophyll / received no light, so photosynthesis could not take place there and no starch was made."

8. Quick Practice Questions

Q1. State the four things a green plant needs for photosynthesis, and the two things it produces.

Answer: It needs water, carbon dioxide, light and chlorophyll. It produces food (sugar/starch) and oxygen.

Q2. A potted plant is placed in a dark cupboard for two weeks. Explain what happens to it.

Answer: Without light, photosynthesis cannot take place. The plant cannot make food, so it will not have enough energy to grow, and it will eventually turn yellow and die.

Q3. Why does a plant kept in a sealed transparent jar with no air eventually stop growing?

Answer: Without carbon dioxide, photosynthesis cannot take place, so the plant cannot make food.

Q4. Explain why a leaf is broad and flat.

Answer: The broad, flat shape gives the leaf a large surface area to absorb more light for photosynthesis.

Q5. Ali says, "Plants take in carbon dioxide and give out oxygen all the time." Is he correct? Explain.

Answer: No. Plants only take in carbon dioxide and give out oxygen when they photosynthesise, which needs light. At night, there is no light, so plants only respire — they take in oxygen and give out carbon dioxide.

Q6. Why must a plant be kept in the dark for two days before a starch test experiment?

Answer: To remove any starch already stored in the leaves, so that any starch found after the experiment must have been made during the experiment.

Q7. Why are green plants called producers?

Answer: Green plants are producers because they are able to make their own food by photosynthesis. All animals depend on plants for food, directly or indirectly.

9. One-Page Revision Summary

  1. Photosynthesis: green plants make their own food using water + carbon dioxide + light + chlorophyll.
  2. It produces food (sugar/starch) and oxygen.
  3. Water ← roots (from soil). Carbon dioxide ← air, through the stomata. Light ← Sun, trapped by chlorophyll.
  4. Roots absorb water and mineral salts — NOT food. Plants make food; they don't absorb it!
  5. The leaf is the food factory: broad and flat (large surface area for light), green (chlorophyll traps light), thin, with stomata.
  6. Remove any one need → photosynthesis cannot take place → no food → the plant dies.
  7. Plants respire all the time, day and night. Photosynthesis happens only in light. At night: respiration only.
  8. Starch test: iodine turns blue-black = starch = photosynthesis took place. Destarch the plant first by keeping it in the dark.
  9. Photosynthesis matters: food for all living things, gives out oxygen, and removes carbon dioxide from the air.
